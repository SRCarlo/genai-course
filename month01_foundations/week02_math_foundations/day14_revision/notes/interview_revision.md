# Day 14 Interview Revision Questions and Answers

## 1. What is Linear Algebra?

Linear Algebra is a branch of mathematics used to represent and manipulate data using vectors and matrices. It forms the foundation of Artificial Intelligence and Machine Learning.

---

## 2. What is a Vector?

A vector is an ordered collection of numbers representing data in a mathematical form.

Example:

```python
[10, 20, 30]
```

---

## 3. What is a Matrix?

A matrix is a collection of numbers arranged in rows and columns.

Example:

```python
[
 [1, 2],
 [3, 4]
]
```

---

## 4. Why is Linear Algebra Important in AI?

AI models process data as vectors and matrices. Operations such as embeddings, neural networks, and image processing rely heavily on Linear Algebra.

---

## 5. What is Probability?

Probability is a measure of the likelihood that an event will occur.

Formula:

```text
Probability =
Favorable Outcomes
-------------------
Total Outcomes
```

---

## 6. Why is Probability Important in AI?

Probability helps AI systems make predictions and decisions under uncertainty.

---

## 7. What is Statistics?

Statistics is the science of collecting, analyzing, and interpreting data.

---

## 8. What is Mean?

Mean is the average value of a dataset.

Formula:

```text
Mean = Sum of Values / Number of Values
```

---

## 9. What is Median?

Median is the middle value in a sorted dataset.

---

## 10. What is Mode?

Mode is the most frequently occurring value in a dataset.

---

## 11. What is Variance?

Variance measures how far data points are spread from the mean.

---

## 12. What is Standard Deviation?

Standard Deviation is the square root of variance and measures data consistency.

---

## 13. What is an Outlier?

An outlier is a data point significantly different from other values in a dataset.

---

## 14. What is Calculus?

Calculus is the branch of mathematics that studies change and motion.

---

## 15. What is a Function?

A function maps inputs to outputs according to a rule.

Example:

```text
f(x) = x²
```

---

## 16. What is a Derivative?

A derivative measures how quickly a function changes with respect to its input.

---

## 17. What is a Gradient?

A gradient is a collection of derivatives that indicates the direction of maximum increase.

---

## 18. Why is Calculus Important in AI?

Calculus helps models understand errors and optimize parameters during learning.

---

## 19. What is Gradient Descent?

Gradient Descent is an optimization algorithm used to minimize loss by updating model parameters.

---

## 20. What is Learning Rate?

Learning Rate controls the size of updates during Gradient Descent.

---

## 21. What is Machine Learning?

Machine Learning is a field of AI where systems learn patterns from data and make predictions without explicit programming.

---

## 22. What is a Dataset?

A dataset is a collection of data used for training and testing Machine Learning models.

---

## 23. What is a Feature?

A feature is an input variable used by a Machine Learning model.

Example:

```text
Hours Studied
```

---

## 24. What is a Target Variable?

A target variable is the output the model tries to predict.

Example:

```text
Exam Score
```

---

## 25. What is Training in Machine Learning?

Training is the process of learning patterns from historical data.

---

## 26. What is Prediction?

Prediction is the output generated by a trained Machine Learning model.

---

## 27. What is Error in Machine Learning?

Error is the difference between actual values and predicted values.

---
